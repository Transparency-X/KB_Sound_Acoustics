# Sound, Resonance & Acoustic Violence Taxonomy v2

This document is a comprehensive, interdisciplinary glossary of terms related to sound, resonance, acoustic harassment, and sonic weaponry. It is organised into 11 thematic categories and cross-references related concepts. Each entry includes a concise definition and, where appropriate, a note on the scientific evidence level.

---

## 1. Sound Fundamentals

The physical properties of sound and basic acoustics.

- **Acoustics**: The branch of physics dealing with the generation, propagation, and reception of sound.
- **Frequency (Hz)**: The number of complete wave cycles per second; determines the perceived pitch.
- **Amplitude / Sound Pressure Level (dB)**: Objective measure of sound pressure relative to a reference; often A‑weighted (dBA) to approximate human hearing sensitivity.
- **Wavelength**: The physical distance between successive pressure peaks of a sound wave.
- **Sound Power vs. Sound Pressure**: Sound power is the total acoustic energy radiated by a source (watts); sound pressure is the local pressure variation (dB) measured at a distance.
- **Harmonics**: Integer multiples of a fundamental frequency; contribute to timbre.
- **Overtones**: All resonant frequencies above the fundamental, including harmonics and sometimes non‑harmonic partials.
- **Standing Waves**: Wave patterns created by interference of two identical waves travelling in opposite directions, resulting in fixed nodes (silence) and antinodes (maxima).
- **Beat Frequency**: The amplitude modulation heard when two tones of slightly different frequencies interfere; the beat rate equals the absolute difference in Hz.
- **Doppler Effect**: The shift in observed frequency when a sound source and observer move relative to each other.
- **Decibel Scales & Weighting (dBA, dBC, dBZ)**: A‑weighting attenuates low and very high frequencies to mirror human loudness perception; C‑weighting is flatter, used for high‑level impulsive sounds; Z‑weighting is unweighted.
- **Time‑Weighted Average (TWA) & Exposure**: An 8‑hour equivalent continuous sound level (Leq,8h) used in occupational health to assess risk of hearing damage, often limited to 85 dBA (NIOSH). Peak SPL limits also apply for impulsive noise.
- **Impulse & Impact Noise**: Sudden, high‑intensity sounds of short duration (e.g., gunfire, explosions); particularly damaging due to rapid pressure rise.

## 2. Psychoacoustics & Perception

How the human auditory system interprets sound; individual variability and subjective responses.

- **Loudness (sone, phon)**: The subjective perception of sound intensity; a phon scale uses dB SPL at 1 kHz as reference, while the sone scale is linear. `[See also: Amplitude]`
- **Pitch**: The perceived highness or lowness of a tone, primarily determined by frequency, but also influenced by intensity and duration.
- **Timbre**: The characteristic quality of a sound that distinguishes different sources (e.g., a violin vs. a flute), even when pitch and loudness are identical.
- **Masking**: When the audibility threshold of a target sound is raised by the presence of another sound (masker). Includes simultaneous, forward, and backward masking.
- **Binaural Beats**: An auditory illusion perceived when two tones with slightly different frequencies are presented separately to each ear. The brain “hears” a beat at the difference frequency. Evidence for clinical effects (e.g., focus, relaxation) is mixed. `[moderate evidence, not mind control]`
- **Spatial Hearing & HRTF (Head‑Related Transfer Function)**: The cues (interaural time difference, interaural level difference, spectral shaping by the pinna) used to localise sound in 3D space.
- **Auditory Scene Analysis**: The process by which the brain segregates and groups incoming sound streams into meaningful perceptual units (e.g., following one voice in a crowd).
- **Annoyance & Noise Sensitivity**: Annoyance is a negative emotional response to sound; noise sensitivity is a stable personality trait that predisposes individuals to react more strongly to noise.
- **Hyperacusis**: Reduced tolerance to ordinary environmental sounds; perceived as uncomfortably loud even at moderate levels.
- **Misophonia**: A strong aversion to specific sounds (e.g., chewing, breathing), often accompanied by anger or anxiety.
- **Startle & Orienting Response**: Involuntary physiological reaction (increased heart rate, muscle tension) to an abrupt, intense, or unexpected sound. `[See also: Acoustic Violence]`
- **Phon**: Unit of loudness level; the dB SPL of a 1 kHz tone judged equally loud.
- **Sone**: Linear unit of loudness; 1 sone corresponds to 40 phons.
- **Shepard Tone**: An auditory illusion of a continuously ascending (or descending) pitch, created by overlapping octave‑spaced tones. Can induce a sense of unease.

## 3. Resonance & Vibration

How sound energy interacts with objects, cavities, structures, and the human body.

- **Sympathetic Resonance**: Vibration of a passive object when exposed to a sound wave at its natural frequency (e.g., a tuning fork exciting a piano string).
- **Helmholtz Resonance**: Air resonance in a cavity with a narrow opening; the cavity acts as a spring‑mass system. Examples: blowing across a bottle top, room modes.
- **Room Modes**: Standing waves in an enclosed space, occurring at frequencies where the dimensions match multiples of half‑wavelengths (axial, tangential, oblique modes).
- **Structural Resonance**: Vibration of a solid structure at its natural frequency, potentially leading to excessive motion or failure if damping is insufficient.
- **Resonant Frequency / Natural Modes**: The frequencies at which an object or system vibrates most readily; determined by mass, stiffness, and boundary conditions.
- **Damping**: Dissipation of vibrational energy, reducing resonance amplitude. Critical for controlling structural vibration.
- **Cymatics**: Visualisation of sound‑induced vibration patterns, often using fine particles on a plate or membrane.
- **Vibroacoustic Coupling**: The mutual interaction between a flexible structure and the sound field inside an enclosure; critical in vehicle cabins and building acoustics.
- **Whole‑Body Vibration (WBV)**: Low‑frequency mechanical vibration transmitted to the human body (e.g., through a seat or floor). Resonance of internal organs can occur: eyeball ~18–20 Hz, chest wall ~60 Hz, spine ~5 Hz. Relevant to infrasonic exposure claims. `[See also: Infrasonic Weapons, Vibroacoustic Disease]`

## 4. Environmental & Occupational Noise – Health and Social Impact

Noise as a public health stressor; non‑auditory effects, dose‑response relationships, and inequality.

- **Noise‑Induced Hearing Loss (NIHL)**: Permanent threshold shift due to chronic exposure to loud noise; typically begins at 4 kHz notch; regulated by workplace standards.
- **Temporary Threshold Shift (TTS)**: Short‑term hearing impairment following exposure to loud noise, with recovery over hours/days. Repeated TTS can lead to permanent NIHL.
- **Auditory Effects**: Includes tinnitus (ringing in the ears), hyperacusis, and difficulty with speech‑in‑noise.
- **Non‑Auditory Health Effects**: Evidence‑based links to hypertension, ischaemic heart disease, sleep disturbance, cognitive impairment in children, and elevated stress hormones (WHO Environmental Noise Guidelines).
- **Cardiovascular Outcomes**: Chronic exposure to road traffic or aircraft noise ≥55 dB Lden is associated with increased risk of myocardial infarction and stroke.
- **Sleep Disturbance**: Noise‑induced arousals and sleep‑stage changes; long‑term exposure linked to metabolic and psychiatric disorders.
- **Cognitive Impairment**: Studies show reduced reading comprehension and memory in children attending schools under flight paths.
- **Vibroacoustic Disease (VAD)**: A controversial but documented pathological condition involving thickening of the pericardium, respiratory dysfunction, and neurological symptoms attributed to chronic exposure to low‑frequency noise (<500 Hz) over years, particularly in aircraft technicians and disco workers. `[limited but serious case evidence]` `[See also: Whole‑Body Vibration]`
- **Low‑Frequency Noise (LFN) Syndrome**: Persistent complaints of an inaudible “hum”, often accompanied by headache, nausea, and distress; source may be industrial machinery, gas pipelines, or unidentified. Sleep loss and psychological factors complicate diagnosis.
- **Environmental Noise Inequality / Environmental Justice**: Disproportionate noise burden on low‑income, minority, and marginalised communities from highways, airports, and industrial sites; recognised as an environmental justice issue.
- **Community Annoyance Dose‑Response**: Well‑established curves relating long‑term average noise level (e.g., Lden) to the percentage of highly annoyed residents; used in policy setting.
- **Occupational Exposure Standards**: OSHA (US) permissible exposure limit 90 dBA TWA 8h; NIOSH recommends 85 dBA TWA 8h with 3 dB exchange rate; EU Physical Agents (Noise) Directive sets 85 dBA LEX,8h.

## 5. Sound as a Weapon, Tool of Harassment, and Torture

Deliberate use of acoustic energy, perception, or sound‑induced stress to harm, coerce, or control.

- **Acoustic Violence**: Umbrella term for any intentional infliction of harm through sound, ranging from weapons to psychological abuse.
- **Long Range Acoustic Device (LRAD)**: A directional sound emitter capable of producing focused tones up to ~162 dB at 1 m. Used for hailing, warning, and crowd control. At close range, can cause permanent hearing damage and vestibular effects.
- **Sonic Cannon / Sound Cannon**: Colloquial term for high‑intensity low‑frequency devices; real‑world implementations are often LRAD‑type arrays or infrasound generators.
- **Infrasonic Weapons**: Systems designed to project frequencies below 20 Hz. Claims include nausea, disorientation, and organ resonance. Early research (e.g., Gavreau, Cold War programs) demonstrated discomfort but no reliably lethal fielded weapon. `[highly variable effects, no reproducible battlefield lethality]`
- **Ultrasonic Weapons**: Sound above 20 kHz; can cause localised heating, pain from high‑intensity focused ultrasound, or covert communication. Investigated in Havana Syndrome hypotheses.
- **Sonic Boom as a Weapon**: Low‑altitude supersonic flight over populated areas to induce startle, glass breakage, and psychological intimidation; used in conflict zones.
- **Sound Harassment / Acoustic Stalking**: The systematic use of noise (bass tones, white noise, directed whispers) to disrupt sleep, cause anxiety, and make a target’s life unbearable. Often reported by individuals in stalking or neighbour disputes.
- **Psychological Acoustics in Interrogation**: Deployment of loud, dissonant, or repetitious sound (music, noise) to cause sleep deprivation, sensory disorientation, and psychological regression. Documented in CIA and military interrogations.
- **Acoustic Surveillance**: Use of laser microphones (reflecting laser off a window to detect vibrations) or ultrasonic beacons to covertly monitor speech or movement.
- **Tinnitus Induction**: Deliberate exposure to high‑intensity impulsive or steady noise to cause permanent tinnitus.
- **Havana Syndrome / Anomalous Health Incidents**: Series of unexplained neurological symptoms (vertigo, cognitive dysfunction, headache) reported by diplomats and intelligence officers since 2016. Suspected directed energy source (pulsed RF/ultrasound), but consensus remains elusive. `[active investigation]` `[See also: Ultrasonic Weapons]`
- **Historical & Cultural Sound Weapons**: Aztec death whistle (terrifying scream sound), Jericho trumpets on Stuka dive bombers (psychological warfare), biblical shofar (intimidation), Japanese “war tubas” (acoustic detection, not weapons), acoustic mines (WW2, triggered by ship’s noise).
- **Misuse of Therapeutic/Consumer Tech**: Repurposing white‑noise machines for constant masking harassment, ultrasonic pest repellers aimed at humans, personal massagers used as infrasound sources.
- **Noise as Torture**: Loud music and white noise have been used in detention settings (e.g., Abu Ghraib, Guantánamo) as part of “enhanced interrogation”, violating the UN Convention Against Torture. `[See also: Legal & Ethical Dimensions]`
- **Sonic Deterrents (Civilian Misuse)**: Devices like the Mosquito (youth deterrent) that emit a high‑frequency tone (~17.5 kHz) annoyingly perceptible to young people; deployment in public spaces raises ethical and legal questions.
- **Fear Frequency / Infrasound Ghost Hypothesis**: Urban legend and pop‑culture claims that specific frequencies (e.g., 18.98 Hz) reliably induce fear. Lab evidence shows infrasound may cause unease in some contexts but is not a predictable “fear button”. `[unreliable claim; not scientifically robust]`

## 6. Military & Security Applications

Institutionalised, state‑sponsored acoustics for defence, intelligence, and control.

- **Acoustic Warfare**: Full‑spectrum operations encompassing detection, localisation, communication jamming, deception, and weaponised sound.
- **LRAD / Acoustic Hailing Devices (AHDs)**: Military and police use for escalation of force, perimeter security, and clearing buildings. Often mounted on ships for anti‑piracy.
- **Sniper Detection Systems**: Gunshot localisation using microphone arrays (e.g., Boomerang, ShotSpotter military) to detect shooter position in urban combat.
- **Infrasound Monitoring for Nuclear Tests**: The Comprehensive Nuclear‑Test‑Ban Treaty Organization (CTBTO) uses infrasound arrays globally to detect atmospheric explosions.
- **Speech Privacy & Sound Masking**: Active systems generating speech‑shaped noise to render conversations unintelligible in Sensitive Compartmented Information Facilities (SCIFs).
- **Sonic Deception**: Historical use of loudspeakers to broadcast tank and troop movement sounds (e.g., the “Ghost Army” in WW2) to mislead enemy forces.
- **Acoustic Mines & Torpedoes**: Mines detonated by the acoustic signature of a ship; modern torpedoes use passive/active acoustic homing.
- **Acoustic Non‑Lethal Weapons (NLW)**: LRADs and similar devices classified as NLW; their use must comply with international humanitarian law (proportionality, precaution).
- **Underwater Sonar & Impact on Marine Life**: Active sonar used by navies can cause decompression sickness, stranding, and hearing damage in cetaceans; a point of intersection between military acoustics and environmental impact.

## 7. Psychological & Behavioural Influence

Sound‑based techniques that shape emotion, cognition, and action, from benign to manipulative.

- **Music‑Induced Emotion & Retail Manipulation**: Using tempo, volume, and genre to influence shopping pace, dwell time, and purchasing behaviour (Muzak, soundscapes).
- **Subliminal Messaging in Audio**: Purported influence by messages below the threshold of conscious perception. Controlled studies show minimal to no effect on complex behaviour; masked priming can work at very short time scales but not for directive control. `[weak evidence for real‑world influence]`
- **Brainwave Entrainment (Binaural Beats, Isochronic Tones)**: Attempts to synchronise brain electrical activity to an external rhythmic stimulus. Modest evidence exists for relaxation/focus induction, but not “mind control”. `[See also: Binaural Beats]`
- **Acoustic Hypnosis / Trance Induction**: Repetitive drumming, chanting, and rhythmic sound are used in traditional and clinical contexts to facilitate trance states.
- **Audio Stroboscopic / Flicker Effects**: Rhythmic light and sound pulses at frequencies between 1–25 Hz can precipitate seizures in photosensitive individuals; weaponisation potential (e.g., “flicker vertigo”) explored.
- **Infrasound and Emotion**: Frequencies around 17–20 Hz may elicit feelings of awe, unease, or chills in certain settings (e.g., church organs, “haunted” locations), but the effect is highly context‑dependent and not reliable. `[unreliable as a sole cause of fear]` `[See also: Fear Frequency]`
- **Sound and Consumer Behaviour**: Sonic branding (Netflix “ta‑dum”, Intel jingle) creates brand recall; volume manipulation in bars leads to faster drinking and higher turnover.
- **Audio Pareidolia**: Hearing intelligible voices or messages in random noise (e.g., EVP, reverse‑speech myths); a normal tendency of the brain to seek patterns.

## 8. Cultural, Spiritual & Artistic Sound

The symbolic, ritual, and aesthetic dimensions of sound.

- **Sacred Sound & Ritual**: Use of chanting, mantras, bells, and specific instruments in religious ceremonies; acoustic properties of churches, mosques, and temples designed to enhance transcendence.
- **Sound Healing / Vibrational Therapy**: Use of singing bowls, tuning forks, gongs, and specific frequencies to promote relaxation and well‑being. Clinical evidence is mixed; placebo and relaxation responses are significant.
- **Cymatics in Art & Education**: Visualising vibration patterns as a means of creative expression and science communication.
- **Soundscape Ecology & Composition**: R. Murray Schafer’s framework: biophony (animal sounds), geophony (wind, water, earth), anthrophony (human‑generated noise). Field recording as art and conservation advocacy.
- **Sound Art & Installation**: Works that treat sound as a primary medium; often site‑specific and immersive.
- **Sonic Branding / Sound Marks**: Registered trademarks consisting of a sound (e.g., MGM lion roar, Harley‑Davidson exhaust note) protected under intellectual property law.
- **Sound Symbolism / Phonosematics**: The association of certain sounds with shapes, sizes, or meanings, e.g., “bouba” (soft, round) vs. “kiki” (sharp, spiky).
- **Acoustic Ecology Activism**: Efforts to preserve natural quiet and reduce anthropogenic noise in wilderness areas and urban spaces.

## 9. Technology & Engineering

Applications of acoustic principles in devices, materials, and processing.

- **Active Noise Control (ANC)**: Electronic cancellation of unwanted sound by generating an anti‑phase signal; used in headphones, vehicles, and duct systems.
- **Soundproofing & Acoustic Absorption**: Use of mass, damping materials, porous absorbers, and Helmholtz resonators to block or absorb sound.
- **Architectural Acoustics & Room Design**: Managing reverberation time (RT60), speech intelligibility (STI), and sound isolation in concert halls, lecture rooms, and studios.
- **Sonar & Acoustic Imaging**: Active/passive sonar for underwater detection; medical ultrasound (imaging and therapy); acoustic cameras for industrial leak detection.
- **Parametric / Directional Speakers**: Emit a highly collimated beam of audible sound by exploiting nonlinear propagation of ultrasound; enable focused audio messages in public spaces (“audio spotlight”).
- **Acoustic Metamaterials**: Engineered sub‑wavelength structures that can manipulate sound in exotic ways: cloaking, superlensing, extraordinary absorption.
- **Acoustic Levitation**: Standing wave pressure nodes used to suspend small objects in mid‑air; has applications in containerless processing.
- **Photoacoustic & Thermoacoustic Effects**: Generation of sound from absorbed modulated light (photoacoustic imaging) or from temperature gradients (thermoacoustic engines and coolers).
- **Bioacoustics Technology**: Passive acoustic monitoring of biodiversity, species identification via machine learning, and analysis of animal communication (e.g., whale song).
- **Assistive Listening & Sonification**: Hearing loops, IR systems, and the translation of data into non‑speech audio for accessibility or data exploration (e.g., NASA’s sonifications of space images).
- **Ultrasonic Communication & Data Transmission**: Acoustic data transfer through air or solids using inaudible frequencies (e.g., device pairing, proximity marketing).

## 10. Legal, Ethical & Human Rights Dimensions

Regulation, liability, and the moral landscape of sound use.

- **Noise Ordinances & Environmental Law**: Municipal codes setting maximum permissible noise levels (e.g., L10, Lmax) by time of day and zoning; often enforced through complaint‑based systems.
- **Occupational Noise Regulations**: Legal obligations to measure, control, and protect workers from excessive noise; includes mandatory hearing conservation programs.
- **Acoustic Torture & Ill‑Treatment**: The use of sound as torture is unequivocally prohibited under international law (UN Convention Against Torture, Geneva Conventions, International Criminal Court). Cases from Abu Ghraib, Guantánamo, and elsewhere confirm its use.
- **Proportionality of Acoustic NLW**: Under international humanitarian law, the use of LRADs and similar devices must not cause superfluous injury or unnecessary suffering; permanent deafness could render an otherwise lawful weapon disproportionate.
- **Right to Acoustic Privacy**: European Court of Human Rights has ruled that severe, persistent neighbour noise can violate Article 8 (right to respect for private life). Many jurisdictions recognise “private nuisance” in tort law.
- **Environmental Noise as Human Rights Issue**: Excessive environmental noise may interfere with rights to health, adequate housing, and a healthy environment; legal challenges increasingly target airports and highway expansions.
- **Copyright & Sound Marks**: Legal protection of recorded works and sonic brand identifiers under intellectual property law (US Lanham Act, EU trade mark regulation).
- **Ethics of Directed Sound Advertising**: Hypersound parametric speakers enable audio that only a single person hears; raises concerns about consent, manipulation, and intrusion.
- **Informed Consent in Acoustic Research**: Ethical requirement when testing human reactions to potentially disturbing sounds (infrasound, weapons simulants).

## 11. Emerging & Experimental Concepts

Cutting‑edge and speculative ideas that extend the boundaries of acoustics.

- **Quantum Acoustics / Phononics**: Study of phonons (quantised sound/vibration) for quantum information processing; quantum‑like behaviour of acoustic waves in metamaterials.
- **Neuroacoustics (Advanced)**: Mapping how the brain encodes complex sounds, speech, and music; applications in brain‑computer interfaces and diagnosing neurological disorders through auditory‑evoked potentials.
- **Acoustic Holograms & Haptic Sound**: Ultrasonic phased arrays that create tangible 3D pressure spots in the air; enables mid‑air tactile feedback and volumetric displays.
- **Vibroacoustic Therapy (Clinical)**: Use of low‑frequency sinusoidal vibration via a chair or mat for pain management, muscle relaxation, and anxiety reduction; undergoing clinical trials.
- **Sonogenetics**: A new technique analogous to optogenetics, using focused ultrasound to activate genetically modified neurons; currently demonstrated in animal models (e.g., C. elegans, mice) with potential for non‑invasive deep brain stimulation.
- **Deep Learning in Acoustics**: Source separation, noise reduction, anomaly detection (industrial), and generative audio; powerful but also raises concerns about audio deepfakes (voice cloning) and spoofing.
- **Acoustic Cloaking**: Theoretical and experimental designs that steer sound around an object, rendering it acoustically invisible.
- **Space Acoustics / Solar Sound Waves**: Helioseismology studies pressure waves inside the Sun; infrasound in planetary atmospheres (e.g., Mars microphones).

## Connections & Cross‑Cutting Themes

- **Frequency continuum**: The same physics connects infrasound (<20 Hz), audible, and ultrasound (>20 kHz), but perception, health effects, and weapon potential vary dramatically.
- **Dose‑intent nexus**: A sound that is harmless at low dose can become a weapon at high dose; intent transforms environmental noise into acoustic violence.
- **Pseudoscience boundary**: Several terms (fear frequency, subliminal mind control, brown note) persist in popular culture without robust scientific support. This taxonomy marks them clearly.
- **Health rights**: The non‑auditory health consequences of noise (cardiovascular, sleep, cognitive) are as severe as direct hearing damage and raise questions of social justice and environmental law.
